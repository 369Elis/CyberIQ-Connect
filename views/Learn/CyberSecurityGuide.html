An Ultimate Guide to Cyber Security for Beginners

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These cyberattacks are usually aimed at accessing, changing, or destroying sensitive information; extorting money from users; or interrupting normal business processes.

CIA Triad

The CIA Triad refers to the foundational model of cybersecurity, emphasizing three key principles: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. These principles help guide policies for information security within an organization.

Specialties in Cybersecurity

To pursue your career in cybersecurity, it is essential to know about the areas of specialization in it, and this cyber security for beginners tutorial will help you do just that. There are nine:

  • Access control systems and methodology: Protects critical system resources from unauthorized modification.
  • Telecommunications and network security: Covers communications, protocols, and network vulnerabilities.
  • Security management practices: Handles disasters, system failures, and service interruptions.
  • Security architecture and models: Focuses on policy and procedure planning for security issues.
  • Law, investigation, and ethics: Covers legal aspects of cybersecurity.
  • Application and system development security: Involves securing databases and internal applications.
  • Cryptography: Teaches encryption principles and appropriate usage.
  • Computer operations security: Protects systems while they are running.
  • Physical security: Deals with physical access to hardware and devices.

Basic Terminologies

  • Network: A connection between two or more computers that communicate. For example: (image placeholder).
  • Internet: Connects computers globally via routers and servers.
  • IP Address: A numeric identifier for devices on a network (e.g., 168.192.10.9).
  • MAC Address: Unique hardware identifier (e.g., D8-EC-52-H-A3-TO).
  • DNS (Domain Name Server): Acts like the internet's phonebook, translating domain names into IP addresses.
  • DHCP: Automatically assigns IP addresses to devices on a network.
  • Router: Directs data across networks to appropriate destinations.
  • Bots: Programs that run automated tasks and can control systems unknowingly.
