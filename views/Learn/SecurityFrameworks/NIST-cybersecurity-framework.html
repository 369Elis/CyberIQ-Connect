Understanding the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0, released in February 2024, provides organizations with a structured approach to managing cybersecurity risks. It is designed for businesses, government agencies, and institutions of all sizes, helping them assess, prioritize, and improve their cybersecurity posture.

Key Components of CSF 2.0

Core Functions

The framework is built around six key functions:

  • Govern – Establish and oversee cybersecurity strategies, policies, and risk management.
  • Identify – Recognize cybersecurity risks, assets, and vulnerabilities.
  • Protect – Implement safeguards to mitigate security risks.
  • Detect – Continuously monitor systems for potential threats.
  • Respond – Take action to manage and contain incidents.
  • Recover – Restore operations after a cybersecurity event.

Profiles

Organizations create Current and Target Profiles to assess their cybersecurity maturity and plan improvements.

Tiers

Organizations classify their cybersecurity risk management rigor into four levels:

  • Tier 1: Partial – Ad hoc, minimal risk management.
  • Tier 2: Risk-Informed – Awareness exists but processes are not consistently applied.
  • Tier 3: Repeatable – Policies and practices are standardized and continuously improved.
  • Tier 4: Adaptive – Cybersecurity is integrated into enterprise-wide risk management.

Why Use CSF 2.0?

  • Flexible & Scalable – Applicable to all industries and organizations, regardless of size.
  • Risk-Based Approach – Helps prioritize cybersecurity investments.
  • Alignment with Global Standards – Works alongside other cybersecurity frameworks and regulations.
  • Improved Communication – Provides a common language for executives, security teams, and stakeholders.
