Types of Email Attacks

Email is a widely used communication tool, making it a major target for cybercriminals. Various types of email attacks exploit vulnerabilities to steal data, install malware, and conduct fraudulent activities. Below are some common types of email-based cyber threats.

Phishing

Phishing attacks involve deceptive emails that impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords and financial data.

Spear Phishing

A highly targeted attack where cybercriminals research their victims to make phishing emails appear more convincing.

Whaling

Targets high-profile executives or officials, often attempting to manipulate them into authorizing fraudulent transactions.

Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Scammers use phone calls and voice messages to impersonate banks or companies, tricking victims into revealing confidential details.

Smishing (SMS Phishing)

Similar to phishing but conducted through text messages, directing victims to fraudulent websites or installing malware.

Pharming

Redirects users from legitimate websites to counterfeit versions, tricking them into entering personal data.

Spyware & Adware

Spyware monitors user activity and collects sensitive information, while adware displays intrusive advertisements and may carry malware.

Scareware

Fake warnings trick users into downloading malicious software by falsely claiming their device is infected.

Spam & Email-Based Malware

Unsolicited bulk emails that distribute malware, phishing links, and fraudulent schemes.

How to Protect Yourself from Email Attacks

  • Be cautious with emails from unknown sources.
  • Verify links before clicking and check for unusual domain names.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.
  • Keep your operating system and software updated.
  • Avoid urgent requests for personal information in emails.
