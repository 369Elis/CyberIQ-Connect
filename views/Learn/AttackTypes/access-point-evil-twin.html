Evil Twin Attack: The Dangers of Fake WiFi Networks and How to Stay Safe

What Is an Evil Twin Attack?

An Evil Twin attack is a cyber threat where a hacker sets up a fake WiFi hotspot that looks identical to a legitimate public network. Once users unknowingly connect to this fraudulent network, the attacker can monitor their online activity, intercept sensitive data, and even manipulate transactions.

These attacks are particularly dangerous because anyone can execute them, even without advanced hacking skills. With tools like a WiFi Pineapple, attackers can quickly create rogue access points that mimic trusted networks in places like coffee shops, airports, hotels, and libraries.
