Jamming Attacks: How Wireless Networks Are Disrupted and How to Stay Protected

What Is a Jamming Attack?

A jamming attack is a type of cyber threat that disrupts wireless communications by overwhelming a network with interference signals. These attacks can target Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, cellular networks, and GPS systems, preventing devices from connecting and functioning properly.

One alarming example occurred in Mexico in 2020, where 85% of cargo truck thefts reportedly involved GPS jamming, allowing criminals to hijack vehicles without detection.

While some jamming attacks are used in cybercrime and warfare, others can be more localized and disruptive, such as cutting off Wi-Fi signals at a party or disabling security cameras before a break-in.
