Privilege Escalation: A Serious Cybersecurity Risk

What is Privilege Escalation?

Privilege escalation happens when an attacker gains higher-level access within a system, often by exploiting security flaws. With increased privileges, they can modify system settings, steal sensitive data, install malware, or even take complete control of the system.

Computer systems assign different levels of access to users. Regular users typically have limited permissions, while administrators and superusers have full control over critical system functions. If an attacker successfully escalates their privileges, they can bypass security restrictions, disable defenses, and carry out dangerous activities without detection.

The attacker gains entry through phishing, malware, or exploiting system weaknesses.

  • Vertical: Exploiting vulnerabilities to gain admin-level control.
  • Horizontal: Taking over another user‚Äôs account to access their private information.

Establishing Persistence:

Exploitation:

Privilege escalation can happen locally (inside an organization) or remotely (through external cyberattacks), making it a difficult threat to detect.
