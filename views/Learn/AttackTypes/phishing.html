Phishing Attacks

Phishing is one of the most common and effective cyber threats, designed to deceive individuals into revealing sensitive information such as login credentials, financial details, or personal data. Cybercriminals use various phishing techniques to exploit trust and manipulate victims into taking harmful actions.

Types of Phishing Attacks

Email Phishing

Attackers send deceptive emails pretending to be from reputable sources, attempting to trick recipients into clicking on malicious links or downloading malware.

Spear Phishing

A highly targeted phishing attack where cybercriminals research their victims to make fraudulent emails appear more convincing.

Whaling

A phishing attack that specifically targets high-profile executives or officials, often involving fraudulent requests for money transfers or sensitive business information.

Clone Phishing

Attackers duplicate legitimate emails from a trusted sender but replace original links or attachments with malicious versions to deceive recipients into interacting with them.

Vishing (Voice Phishing)

Scammers use phone calls and voice messages to impersonate banks, businesses, or government agencies, manipulating victims into revealing confidential details.

Smishing (SMS Phishing)

A form of phishing conducted through text messages, directing victims to fake websites or installing malware on their mobile devices.

Pharming

A cyber attack that redirects users from legitimate websites to counterfeit versions, tricking them into entering personal information.

Business Email Compromise (BEC)

Attackers impersonate executives, partners, or trusted individuals within an organization to manipulate employees into transferring funds or sharing confidential data.

How to Protect Yourself from Phishing Attacks

  • Be cautious with unexpected emails, especially those requesting sensitive information.
  • Verify links before clicking and check for unusual domain names.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) to enhance security.
  • Keep your operating system and security software updated.
  • Avoid responding to urgent requests for financial transactions via email or phone.
