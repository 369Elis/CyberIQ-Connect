What is a Shoulder Surfing Attack?

A shoulder surfing attack occurs when an individual gains unauthorized access to personal information by observing someone’s screen or keypad. Unlike most cyber threats, this method requires the attacker to be physically near the target.

While it can be as simple as glancing over someone’s shoulder, more advanced attackers might use tools like binoculars, miniature cameras, or other optical devices to spy from a distance. The goal is to capture sensitive data such as login credentials, personal details, or financial information like credit card numbers.

Although many shoulder surfing incidents are deliberate and malicious, some occur due to overly curious individuals invading privacy rather than intending to steal information.

How Shoulder Surfing Attacks Work

The method is typically straightforward—an attacker strategically places themselves to see the victim's screen and keystrokes. As the target enters passwords, PINs, or sensitive information, the attacker memorizes or records the details.

Less sophisticated attackers may jot down information manually, while more advanced ones might employ hidden cameras or other recording devices to capture details discreetly.

It’s important to note that incidents involving skimming devices (such as card readers installed on ATMs) or remote access hacking don’t fall under shoulder surfing, as they do not require direct visual observation.

Examples of Shoulder Surfing in Action

  • ATM PIN Theft: A person discreetly observes someone entering their PIN at an ATM. If the victim leaves the machine without confirming the session has ended, the attacker can use the obtained PIN to conduct additional transactions.
  • Public Transport Risks: In crowded places, attackers can easily glance at screens or overhear sensitive conversations, gaining access to confidential details.
  • Unattended Devices: If a person steps away from their unlocked laptop in a public setting after recently entering their password, an onlooker who has memorized the login credentials can access the device, potentially compromising sensitive data.

How to Protect Yourself from Shoulder Surfing Attacks

  • Minimize password use: The best way to prevent password-based attacks is to eliminate passwords altogether by adopting passwordless authentication methods.
  • Use privacy screen protectors: A privacy screen makes it difficult for anyone nearby to see your display, reducing the likelihood of visual eavesdropping. Some screen protectors even double as glass protection for mobile devices.
  • Stay vigilant in public: Always be conscious of your surroundings, especially in busy locations. Attackers tend to target individuals who appear distracted and unaware.
  • Rely on biometric authentication: Using fingerprint or facial recognition for authentication reduces the risk of PIN or password exposure, as attackers have no visible information to steal.

While most people focus on digital threats, physical security risks like shoulder surfing remain a real concern. Awareness of your environment and proactive security measures can help protect sensitive information from prying eyes.
